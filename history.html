The Beginning

Ultimate Frisbee was invented in 1968 by a group of students at Columbia High School in Maplewood, New Jersey. The students were looking for a new sport to play that would be fun, competitive, and easy to learn. They combined elements of football, soccer, and basketball to create Ultimate Frisbee.

Early Growth

In the 1970s, Ultimate Frisbee began to spread to colleges and universities across the United States. The first intercollegiate Ultimate Frisbee game was played in 1972 between Rutgers and Princeton. By the end of the decade, there were hundreds of college teams playing the sport.

International Expansion

Ultimate Frisbee began to gain popularity internationally in the 1980s and 1990s. The World Flying Disc Federation was founded in 1985 to promote and govern the sport worldwide. Today, Ultimate Frisbee is played in more than 80 countries around the world.

Modern Era

In recent years, Ultimate Frisbee has continued to grow in popularity. The sport has been featured in major sporting events, including the World Games and the Pan American Games. There are now professional leagues, international tournaments, and millions of players around the world.
